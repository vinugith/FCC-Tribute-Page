Amrita Sher-Gil

- The Female Pioneer of Modern Indian Art

a brief bio

Amrita Sher-Gil (1913-1941), often recognised as India’s ‘Frida Kahlo’, is one of the nation’s most celebrated female modern artists. Heavily influenced by Realism, Sher-Gil strived to portray the lives of people living within her local community. She was one of the most gifted artists of the pre-colonial era.

  • Date of Birth: January 30, 1913
  • Place of Birth: Budapest, Kingdom of Hungary
  • Date of Death: December 5, 1941
  • Place of Death: Lahore, British India
  • Periods: Modern art, Post-Impressionism
  • Education: Académie de la Grande Chaumière, École nationale supérieure des Beaux-Arts
  • Spouse: Victor Egan
  • Father: Umrao Singh Sher-Gil Majithia
  • Mother: Marie Antoniette Gottesmann

"Modern art has led me to the comprehension and appreciation of Indian painting and sculpture. It seems paradoxical but I know for certain that had we not come away to Europe, I should perhaps never have realised that a fresco from Ajanta or a small piece of sculpture in the Musee Guimet is worth more than a whole Renaissance."

- In 1934 on her new art form - Sikh Heritage, Amrita Shergil
